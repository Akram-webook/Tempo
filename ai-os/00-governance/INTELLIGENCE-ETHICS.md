# Constitution amendment — Intelligence & Memory Ethics (supreme; extends Article II)
Applies to EVERY feature that learns, remembers, or infers (Performance Memory, Evaluation
Intelligence, Leadership Intelligence, any future "DNA"/pattern system). Non-negotiable.

1. **Support, never surveil.** Tempo analyzes *operational decisions and workflow signals* only —
   to make the product more helpful. It NEVER builds psychological profiles, personality assessments,
   hidden judgments, or productivity-policing of people (leaders or employees).
2. **Evidence-first (Article IV/V).** No inferred pattern, score, or recommendation without
   measurable, cited evidence. If evidence is insufficient, state exactly: "Not enough evidence to
   identify a reliable pattern." Never fabricate achievements, ratings, or behaviors.
3. **Human always decides.** AI prepares evidence/drafts/ranges; the manager/leader makes the call.
   Nothing auto-approves, auto-rates, or auto-promotes. Warnings inform; they never block.
4. **Transparent & explainable.** Every insight shows its factors, sources, and confidence. The
   subject's data is visible to them; "Leadership DNA" means *operational preferences* (preferred
   wording, report format, AI-acceptance rate) — NOT character inference.
5. **Dignity & proportionality.** Growth is highlighted, not just mistakes. Risk timelines exist for
   *early support, never punishment*. Capture the minimum needed for the decision at hand.
6. **Access & visibility.** Memory is gated by the existing access model; per-person evidence is
   never peer-visible. Aggregate org insights are anonymized.
A feature that can't meet all six does not ship. This is the gate for the Intelligence Layer roadmap.
